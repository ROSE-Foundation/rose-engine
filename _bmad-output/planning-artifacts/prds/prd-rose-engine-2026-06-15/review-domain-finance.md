# ROSE Engine PRD — Domain & Feasibility Review

**Reviewer scope:** regulated finance, tokenised securities, fund structuring. This review assesses **domain correctness and feasibility**, not writing quality. References are to `prd.md` and `addendum.md` in this folder.

**Overall verdict:** *Conditional — structurally credible, but two central compliance/mechanism claims are not feasible as written and must be re-scoped before build.* The fund wrapper (Singapore VCC sub-fund, licensed IM, ERC-3643 permissioned tokens, private placement to sophisticated clients) is a coherent, real-world-precedented design, and the off-chain double-entry spine is sound. However, the PRD repeatedly asserts that the **same rules are enforced "in lockstep" on-chain and off-chain**, and the **Model-A principal/yield bright line is one of those rules**. That specific claim is **not implementable on a fungible ERC-3643 token** and is the most serious defect. Secondary defects: the coupled-pair **on-chain coupling mechanism** and the **post-reset re-anchoring math** are undefined, ledger↔chain **dual-write atomicity** is unaddressed, and several **standard regulated-token capabilities are omitted** (burn-on-redemption, forced transfer / recovery / freeze, transfer-agent role).

---

## Findings

### F-1 — Model-A "principal vs yield" is not distinguishable on a fungible on-chain token  — **CRITICAL**
**Location:** FR-19, UJ-3 (§2.3), Glossary "Model-A segregation", §4.3 description, §12, NFR-4, Addendum C.
**Issue:** The PRD's load-bearing claim is that the Model-A bright line ("client-collateral *principal* may never leave the client account; *yield* may") is enforced *both* off-chain (FR-8) *and* on-chain by an ERC-3643 compliance module (FR-19), "encoding the same rule," "in lockstep." An ERC-3643 compliance module is invoked as `canTransfer(from, to, amount)` on a **fungible** token. Fungible units are by definition indistinguishable — there is no on-chain notion of "this unit is principal" vs "this unit is yield." A compliance module **cannot** make the principal/yield determination that Model-A requires. The off-chain ledger *can* (it has typed accounts `CLIENT_COLLATERAL` vs `FEE_INCOME` and can track principal vs yield sub-balances); the chain cannot, on a single fungible token.
**Compounding issue:** It is also unclear whether client-collateral *principal* is even a tokenised on-chain asset at all. The security tokens are the L/S legs of the Rose Note; client principal reads as cash in a segregated client account. If principal is off-chain cash, an "on-chain Model-A compliance module" is enforcing a rule over assets that do not exist on-chain — a category error.
**Suggested fix / open question:** Choose one and state it explicitly:
(a) Keep Model-A as a **purely off-chain ledger invariant** and drop the claim that it is mirrored on-chain (most honest for P0); or
(b) Make principal **non-transferable by construction** on-chain — e.g. principal held in a locked vault / address-frozen via ERC-3643 `freeze`, with yield paid out of a *separate* token class or a separate distribution address that the compliance module whitelists as a permitted source. The "same rule on both sides" framing must be replaced with a precise statement of *which* slice of Model-A each side actually enforces.

### F-2 — On-chain "coupling" of the L/S pair is unspecified and non-trivial — **HIGH**
**Location:** Glossary "Coupled pair"/"L-Token / S-Token", FR-18, FR-6, UJ-1.
**Issue:** ERC-3643 is a **per-token** standard: each token is its own contract with its own Identity Registry and Compliance contract. The cardinal rule "a pair never exists as a single leg; both legs created/modified/closed only together" has **no native ERC-3643 mechanism**. A compliance module sees only *its own* token's transfer context; it cannot atomically condition on a counterpart token's transfer in the same transaction without bespoke cross-contract coordination. "Minted as paired units at equal notional" and "a single-leg mint is impossible" therefore require an **orchestration/wrapper layer** (an issuer contract that mints/burns both atomically, plus per-token modules that block independent transfer) — none of which is described.
**Suggested fix:** Specify the coupling enforcement explicitly: a paired-issuance orchestrator contract for atomic mint/burn, and the mechanism that prevents independent secondary transfer of a single leg (e.g. legs non-transferable except via the orchestrator, or a shared compliance module reading both registries). State that the "never a single leg" invariant is enforced by this layer, not by ERC-3643 itself. Also clarify whether the two legs are ever held by *different* parties (see F-4) — if they cannot be, much of the per-token compliance machinery is unnecessary and a single multi-leg contract (or off-chain leg accounting against one token) may be simpler.

### F-3 — Token quantity vs leg value conflation; what does `balanceOf` represent? — **HIGH**
**Location:** FR-18, FR-10/NFR-9 ("ledger token quantities match on-chain balances"), §4.5 ("hybrid value + quantity"), Glossary "Collateral pool (K)".
**Issue:** An ERC-3643 `balanceOf` is a **fixed quantity** of tokens. But the leg *value* `V_A=(K/2)(1+L·r)` drifts continuously with price and is re-based at each reset. So the on-chain token quantity is *not* the leg value — value is computed off-chain. The PRD mints "at equal notional" and then reconciles "token quantities," which is internally consistent *only* if the token is a fixed share-count claim whose NAV is off-chain. This is workable but never stated, and the surfaces (FR-14: "V_A, V_B … live") imply the chain shows value. The risk is that implementers treat the token as value-bearing and try to re-mint/burn on every price move (gas-prohibitive, breaks the model).
**Suggested fix:** State plainly that the L/S token is a **fixed-quantity claim**; NAV/leg value lives off-chain and is *displayed*, not held, on-chain; reconciliation is quantity-only; resets change the off-chain value mapping, never the on-chain quantity (except at issuance/redemption).

### F-4 — Post-reset re-anchoring math is undefined; "delta-neutral" ≠ "market-neutral"; floor params m, g unspecified and untracked — **HIGH**
**Location:** Addendum D, FR-12, FR-16, SM-2, §11.2 (parked parameters).
**Issues (three, same mechanism):**
1. **Issuer-neutral identity is sound** as accounting: issuer holds pool K, liabilities `V_A+V_B=K`, net 0 — correct, and the real insight (a zero-sum redistribution funded entirely from the shared pool). But the **post-reset dynamics are undefined.** The formula `V_A=(K/2)(1±L·r)` assumes a *symmetric K/2 base*. After a reset, the legs are asymmetric (one near floor `f`, the other near `K−f`) and re-anchored to a new P₀ — the K/2-based formula no longer applies, and the addendum gives no replacement. "Lock current dollar values, re-anchor P₀, loss is locked" does not define the new per-leg base or whether accrued P&L is *crystallised and withdrawable* or *carried*. This is the core mechanic and it is missing. **Who funds the losing leg's lock-in** cannot be answered without it; arithmetically the loss is funded from the shared pool K (zero-sum vs the winning leg), but only if the pool is intact at reset.
2. **"Delta-neutral at issuance" is conflated with "market-neutral."** FR-12: "the embedded position is market-neutral on the underlying; directional risk arises only from strategy." The *issuer* is market-neutral (by the invariant). A *holder of one leg* is fully directional. Whether a Rose Note holder is neutral depends on an unstated fact: **does one Note embed both legs (holder neutral, return comes only from strategy — so the coupled pair is a capital-preservation wrapper, not a return source) or are L and S sold to different holders (each directional, zero-sum between them)?** The economics differ fundamentally. The PRD never says.
3. **Floor parameters `m` (safety margin) and `g` (worst plausible gap) are defined only verbally** (Addendum D) with **no values, no derivation method**, and are **not in the parked-parameters list** (§11.2 lists coupon, use-of-proceeds, conversion, backing-float floor — not m, g). Every reset is triggered by `f=m·L·g`, so the entire rebalancing behaviour depends on two undefined, untracked numbers. Note also that issuer-neutrality is **conditional, not guaranteed**: it holds only if a reset always fires before a leg reaches zero. A gap move past the floor in one tick (BTC flash move, weekend gap) leaves the issuer net short. This conditionality should be stated as the model's primary economic risk.
**Suggested fix:** Add the post-reset leg-value equations to Addendum D; state explicitly whether reset P&L is crystallised or carried and the new per-leg base. Resolve who holds the legs and restate FR-12 distinguishing issuer-neutral from holder-directional. Add `m` and `g` to §11.2 parked parameters (refuse-on-absent). Add a stated assumption that issuer-neutrality holds *only* while resets pre-empt the barrier, and name gap risk as the key model risk in §15.

### F-5 — Ledger↔chain dual-write atomicity and remediation are unaddressed — **HIGH**
**Location:** UJ-1 (§2.3), FR-10, NFR-9, §1 ("off-chain ledger is the accounting source of truth; chain is where the instrument lives").
**Issue:** A mint is two writes in two systems with no shared transaction: an on-chain tx and an off-chain journal entry. There is no two-phase commit across PostgreSQL and an EVM chain. UJ-1's "the database rejects the whole transaction; nothing is half-written" only covers the DB leg. The PRD provides **detection** (reconciliation flags divergence) but **no ordering rule, no idempotency/outbox/saga pattern, no compensation procedure** (you cannot un-mint; you must burn), and **no handling of chain realities** (pending vs finalised tx, failed tx, gas failure, Sepolia reorgs). "Reconcile detects divergence" answers *what if they diverge* with *we'll notice* — not *we'll repair*.
**Compounding legal point:** Declaring the off-chain ledger the "source of truth" for a regulated instrument whose **legal ownership is the on-chain balance** is risky. If the two diverge, the holder's enforceable claim is what the chain says they hold. The PRD should state which record governs *legally* on divergence, separate from which is the *accounting* book of record.
**Suggested fix:** Specify a transactional outbox / saga: define write ordering (typically chain-first, then ledger, with an idempotent reconciler that completes or compensates), idempotency keys per economic event, finality threshold before ledger posting, and an explicit **remediation workflow** for detected divergence (not just a flag). Address reorg handling for Sepolia.

### F-6 — Missing standard regulated-token capabilities — **HIGH**
**Location:** FR-11 (redemption), §6 In Scope, §15, entity list (Glossary "Entity").
**Issue:** Several capabilities that a regulated tokenised-fund product needs — and that ERC-3643 provides for free — are absent:
- **Burn on redemption.** Mint is explicit (FR-18); FR-11 redemption produces *ledger* entries but **never states the on-chain L/S tokens are burned.** Without paired burn, on-chain supply and reconciliation drift on every redemption.
- **Forced transfer / recovery / freeze / pause.** ERC-3643 exposes `forcedTransfer`, address recovery (lost keys), `freeze`/`setAddressFrozen`, and agent-controlled mint/burn — these are *mandatory* for a regulated security token (court orders, sanctions, error correction, lost-key recovery, incident pause). The PRD mentions **none**. This is both an omission and a free capability of the chosen standard.
- **Transfer-agent / fund-administrator role and entity.** The four P0 entities are `VCC, HOLDING, TRADING_CO, ISSUER_EXCHANGE` — there is **no fund administrator / transfer agent / registrar** who maintains the register of members. In a fund this role is structural; on-chain, ERC-3643 agent roles fill it but no party is named.
- **Secondary-transfer compliance dimensions.** Only eligibility + Model-A are modelled. A real private security token typically needs jurisdiction restriction (e.g. Reg S / no-US-persons), holder-count caps, and lock-up modules — none referenced.
**Suggested fix:** Add an FR for paired **burn on redemption** with reconciliation; add an FR/section for **agent powers** (forced transfer, recovery, freeze, pause) and name who holds the agent role; introduce a transfer-agent/administrator role (entity or named function); list the additional compliance modules a real offering will need (even if deferred past P0, name them).

### F-7 — Coupled-pair: ERC-3643 is the right standard, with caveats — **MEDIUM**
**Location:** Glossary "ERC-3643", §1, §12.
**Assessment:** ERC-3643 (T-REX) **is** the correct standard for permissioned, identity-gated security tokens with on-chain compliance — better-suited than ERC-1400/1404 for this use case, and natively matching the "curated allowlist of sophisticated clients" model. ONCHAINID ("on-chain identity carrying eligibility claims"), claim issuer ("trusted party issuing claims to an ONCHAINID"), compliance modules, and the allowlist→Identity-Registry mapping are described **correctly** at the level given. Two omissions worth noting: the **Trusted Issuers Registry** and **Claim Topics Registry** (which make the claim-issuer model actually function) are not mentioned, and **claim expiry / periodic re-verification** of accreditation is not addressed. The unresolved hard problem is not the standard choice but the *coupling* (F-2) and the *Model-A* claim (F-1).
**Suggested fix:** Note the Trusted Issuers / Claim Topics registries in the addendum stack; add claim expiry/renewal to the eligibility model.

### F-8 — "No self-service KYC, curated allowlist" is regulatorily coherent — but allowlist ≠ no KYC/AML — **MEDIUM**
**Location:** §2.2 Non-Users, §5 Non-Goals, Glossary "Allowlist", §12.
**Assessment:** This is **coherent** with VCC/securities practice. A Singapore VCC sub-fund placed privately to accredited/institutional investors (SFA s274/275 exemptions) does **not** require a retail self-service KYC funnel; a curated allowlist of sophisticated clients is exactly how private security-token placements operate, and ERC-3643 is purpose-built for it. **Caveat:** "no self-service KYC" must not be read as "no KYC/AML." Each allowlisted investor still requires full **KYC/AML/CDD and accredited-investor verification** (MAS AML/CFT) performed off-chain *before* the claim issuer issues the eligibility claim — the curation *is* manual KYC, not its absence. The PRD implies counsel handles eligibility criteria (§12 assumption) but never states that allowlisting is gated on completed KYC/AML.
**Suggested fix:** Add one line: allowlist admission is conditioned on completed off-chain KYC/AML and accreditation verification; the claim issuer attests to it. Keep the framing "no self-service KYC *product*," not "no KYC."

### F-9 — Minor: entity-to-structure mapping and source-of-truth wording — **LOW**
**Location:** Glossary "Entity" (`ISSUER_EXCHANGE`) vs Addendum F (Rose Coin Issuer Co. + Rose Offshore Trading Co.).
**Issue:** The four P0 books (`VCC, HOLDING, TRADING_CO, ISSUER_EXCHANGE`) collapse the addendum's legal structure (separate Coin Issuer Co. in Jurisdiction 2 and Trading Co. in Jurisdiction 1, plus the exchange/treasury function). `ISSUER_EXCHANGE` appears to merge coin issuance and exchange. Harmless for P0 books but worth a one-line note so the ledger entities are not mistaken for the legal-entity map.
**Suggested fix:** Add a mapping note: P0 ledger "entities" are accounting books and do not 1:1 mirror the legal-entity / jurisdiction structure in Addendum F.

---

## What is correct / well-judged (for balance)
- **Standard choice (ERC-3643/T-REX)** is right for permissioned security tokens.
- **Singapore VCC sub-fund + licensed IM + private placement to sophisticated clients** is a real, precedented structure (the Laplace pattern confirms it).
- **Issuer-neutral identity `V_A+V_B=K`** is arithmetically sound as an accounting invariant.
- **Off-chain double-entry, DB-enforced balance, integer smallest-units, NUMERIC for 18-decimal tokens, refuse-on-absent** are all correct, disciplined accounting-engineering choices.
- **Testnet/paper P0 with no real capital** correctly defers live regulatory exposure while proving the loop.
- **Threshold-only (not clock-based) rebalancing** correctly avoids leveraged-ETF volatility decay.

## Top open questions for the team
1. (F-1) Is client-collateral principal an on-chain asset at all? If not, drop "on-chain Model-A." If yes, by what mechanism is principal made non-transferable while yield moves?
2. (F-4) Does one Rose Note embed *both* legs (holder market-neutral, return from strategy only) or are L/S sold separately (holders directional, zero-sum)?
3. (F-4) What are the post-reset leg-value equations, and is reset P&L crystallised or carried? What are `m` and `g`?
4. (F-5) On ledger↔chain divergence, which record governs *legally* — and what is the repair procedure, not just the detection?
5. (F-6) Where are burn-on-redemption and the ERC-3643 agent powers (forced transfer, recovery, freeze, pause)? Who holds the agent/transfer-agent role?
