# Story 6.2: Live subscription to a Rose Note end-to-end (testnet/paper)

Status: done

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As a Subscriber (Rose Member),
I want to subscribe to a Rose Note in fiat or crypto and have tokens issued to me,
so that I can participate in the live loop on testnet/paper (FR-11).

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Given** an allowlist-eligible Subscriber with a valid ONCHAINID claim, **when** they subscribe to a Rose Note at the VCC level (fiat or crypto), **then** the subscription drives the paired mint (Epic 5) and produces balanced journal entries touching the appropriate accounts (incl. `NOTE_LIABILITY`), respecting the chokepoint and segregation rules.
2. **Given** a non-eligible would-be subscriber, **when** they attempt to receive tokens, **then** the on-chain eligibility rule rejects it (FR-19); the full subscribe → issue → mint → ledger loop runs end-to-end on Sepolia/paper at small scale (SM-1).
3. **Given** the Subscriber surface (UX-DR6), **when** an eligible Subscriber subscribes, **then** the action passes a **Review → Confirm** panel stating the amount, the embedded coupled pair, and the on-chain consequence, and stays **pending until the on-chain commit point** (no optimistic success) **and** when the eligibility claim is absent/expired, the subscribe path is unavailable with an explicit reason (no self-service KYC), not a generic block.

2. **(Med) Idempotency-key collision returned a stranger's position** — a reused `idempotencyKey` with a different amount/subscriber/note silently returned the ORIGINAL intent as a 201 (the outbox `onConflictDoNothing` discards the new payload). Regression: reused key + different amount → 409.
3. **(Med) `confirm` could throw into the never-throw watcher** — `buildSubscriptionMintPlan` ran outside any try/catch, so a malformed `PairMinted` (non-positive on-chain amount) threw `InvalidSubscriptionAmountError` out of `confirm`, violating the architecture's fire-and-forget-watcher contract. 4. **(Med) Authorization DENY mapped to 422, not 403** — a default-deny capital-flow refusal on a subscribe became `MintAuthorizationError(effect:'DENY')`, which the registry flattened to 422, contradicting the `TransferRefusedError` DENY→403 / REFUSE→422 taxonomy (UX-DR5).
