# Story 5.6: Reconcile ledger↔chain and correct the ledger toward the chain

Status: done

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As an internal operator / steward,
I want reconciliation to detect a ledger↔chain token-quantity divergence (and a per-entity/consolidated inconsistency) and CORRECT the ledger toward the chain on a token-ownership mismatch via a journaled, balanced, double-entry correcting entry,
so that the chain remains the source of truth (D3) and the off-chain books are kept honest and auditable (FR-10, NFR-9).

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Given** a per-entity / consolidated inconsistency, **when** reconciliation runs, **then** the divergence is REPORTED explicitly (the consolidated per-`(asset, scale)` double-entry balance check from the group view is surfaced in the reconciliation report; any `balanced === false` row is flagged, never swallowed).
2. **Given** a deliberately introduced ledger↔chain token-quantity mismatch (the ledger ASSET-side quantity for a token `≠` the authoritative on-chain `totalSupply`), **when** reconciliation runs, **then** the mismatch is REPORTED **and** the ledger is CORRECTED to match the chain via a JOURNALED correcting entry (P0 acceptance criterion). The correcting entry: (a) is a balanced double-entry recorded through `@rose/ledger` `recordJournalEntry` (the DB double-entry trigger is the non-bypassable backstop); (b) adjusts the token's ASSET-side holder account toward the chain quantity, balanced against a caller-supplied non-ASSET contra account in the SAME `(asset, scale)` (mirroring the mint holder/supply-contra topology) so the ledger circulating quantity becomes exactly the on-chain `totalSupply`; (c) carries a human-readable `description` that names the correction + the signed divergence (auditable, never a silent mutation); (d) uses positive integer smallest-unit amounts only (NFR-2 — never a binary float). After the correction, re-deriving the ledger ASSET-side quantity equals the on-chain `totalSupply` (chain authoritative, D3).
3. **Given** an ALREADY chain-consistent ledger (no token diverges) **when** reconciliation runs, **then** NO correcting entry is posted (idempotence): the `journal_entries` count is unchanged and the report shows `anyCorrected === false`. Running reconcile a SECOND time immediately after a correction is likewise a no-op (the first run made the ledger consistent).
4. **Given** the configured reconciliation cadence, **when** a confirmed mint/burn/transfer occurs (per-event) OR `reconcile` is invoked on-demand, **then** reconciliation runs; and a reorg below the configured Sepolia confirmation depth is treated as a reconciliation event that re-derives ledger token quantities from the (new) authoritative chain state. This is delivered as PURE, documented finality/cadence helpers (`isFinal`, a reorg classifier, and an on-event trigger decision) operating on plain block-coordinate data, proven LOCALLY; the default cadence (per-event + on-demand, act at a configured confirmation depth, reorg-below-depth ⇒ reconcile) is documented (architecture §reconciliation-cadence, PRD §8 Q6).

### The reconcile-and-correct math (the load-bearing detail)

For each token `(asset, scale)` in the authoritative `ChainSupplySnapshot`:

- `ledgerQuantity = Σ over accounts a where a.asset === asset AND a.scale === scale AND ACCOUNT_NAV_CLASSIFICATION[a.type].navRole === 'ASSET'` of `(a.debit − a.credit)` — the 5.5 definition (circulating supply held in ASSET accounts).
- `divergence = onChainTotalSupply − ledgerQuantity` (signed; chain authoritative).
- `divergence === 0n` ⇒ consistent ⇒ post nothing (idempotence).
- `divergence > 0n` (chain has MORE than the ledger records): the ledger UNDER-records ⇒ DEBIT `holderAccountId` by `divergence`, CREDIT `contraAccountId` by `divergence`. New `ledgerQuantity = old + divergence = onChainTotalSupply`. ✓
- `divergence < 0n` (chain has LESS): the ledger OVER-records ⇒ CREDIT `holderAccountId` by `-divergence`, DEBIT `contraAccountId` by `-divergence`. New `ledgerQuantity = old − (−divergence) = old + divergence = onChainTotalSupply`. ✓
- Both branches: exactly 2 postings, same `(asset, scale)`, equal positive amount ⇒ `recordJournalEntry` passes its balance check and the DB trigger backstop. After the entry, re-deriving `ledgerQuantity` equals `onChainTotalSupply` — assert this in the test (chain authoritative, D3).

- **AC-2 (detect AND correct toward the chain — P0):** `reconcileLedgerToChain(db, snapshot, plan)` computes per-token `divergence = onChainTotalSupply − ledgerQuantity` (ledgerQuantity = Σ over ASSET-classified accounts of the token `(asset, scale)` of debit−credit — the 5.5 definition) and, for each diverged token with a caller-supplied `TokenCorrectionAccounts` mapping, posts ONE balanced double-entry correcting entry via `@rose/ledger` `recordJournalEntry`: `divergence > 0` ⇒ DEBIT holder + CREDIT contra; `divergence < 0` ⇒ CREDIT holder + DEBIT contra; same `(asset, scale)`, equal positive integer amount ⇒ balanced (the Story-1.5 DB trigger is the non-bypassable backstop). After the correction, re-deriving the ledger ASSET-side quantity equals the on-chain `totalSupply` (tests assert `holderNet === totalSupply` for both a `+200` and a `−150` mismatch).
